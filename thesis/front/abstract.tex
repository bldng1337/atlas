\chapter{Abstract}


Generating realistic landscapes for applications like video games is often limited by a lack
of control over geographic details. This bachelor’s thesis investigates how heightmaps
generated by the diffusion model MESA~\cite{borne-pons2025mesa} can be precisely controlled to create geographically accurate landscapes. An evaluation of ControlNet~\cite{zhang2023controlnet}
for integration into MESA is conducted. Training and evaluation was performed on
the Major\_TOM dataset~\cite{schulz2024majortom} using algorithmically extracted control maps. The goal
is to achieve improved controllability and accuracy of generated landscapes through
additional control signals, going beyond text-based guidance.
